### Support to projects receiving attestations' requests

We started the conversation by identifying the market reality for attestations.

- We know that Curl has not received any attestation requests from companies, suggesting manufacturers are handling CRA compliance internally without engaging upstream projects.  
- Companies and OSPOs lack incentive to act on attestations without clear pain points or cost savings from CRA certification processes.  
- Hen-and-egg problem: No automated validation methods exist, so no demand for attestations; without demand, no validation infrastructure develops.  
- Article 25 is fully optional  
- The reality is that manufacturers must take responsibility for open source components they integrate, regardless of CRA's open source exemptions.  
- It was shared the experience from rail, where early industry feedback shows manufacturers initially claimed open source was "out of scope," but later recognised they need assurance that open source code is secure and actively maintained.  
- Key value: Attestations signal that "somebody is looking after security" of a component, saving manufacturers time and work in due diligence

It was proposed a three step plan:

- Identify typical request cases that benefit from attestation support.  
- Build publicly available templates validated to support open source maintainers hosted by ORC.  
- Increase outreach so everyone knows attestations are available.

To successfully develop the plan we need to address several aspects:

- Problems identified:  
  - Supporting maintainers anticipating an attestation request deluge.  
  - Educating manufacturers who haven't yet recognised CRA implications.  
  - Influencing regulators to promote attestations as a compliance pathway.

- The next challenge is clearly define the problem we are solving with the attestations, so we can focus the communication in this direction.
