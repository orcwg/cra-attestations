### Preparation of the joint meeting with the due diligence group

- Timo and Æva   
- WG9 status?   
  - Developing a list of things that could go into due diligence  
  - But that list is focused on commercial component suppliers, very vague (or maybe nothing at all) about third party OSS components  
  - Commission guidance is similarly vague or absent  
- Timo points out the importance of reminding manufacturers that “maintainers and stewards owe you nothing”. Sebastian affirms the importance of this, and contemplates that some maintainers may already be getting legal/demand letters about attestations. Aeva offers to blog about this.  
- Aeva remarks that WG9 meeting is this week, and should include debate about SBOMs and OSS due diligence.   
- Alistair remarks that, in absence of any clarity from WG9, we should still publish good guidance.  
- Salve comments that he’s “been working from the assumption that Steward orgs have a role to play in "protecting" maintainers from "well-meaning" manufacturers doing their due diligence”  
- Aeva polls the room – particularly considering the unknown and unreliable state of WG9’s guidance, is there general consensus to proceed on creating “due diligence” guidance by the members of this group?  
  - Jordan: now that proposed work is clarified, is supportive  
  - Salve: supportive  
  - Greg: also thinks it is a good idea. “In an environment where people feel completely overwhelmed, we should keep it as short as possible.”  
  - Salve: considers the Gartner report for an opinionated stack based on CHAOSS metrics  
    - [https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/sovereignty-requires-rallying-behind-free-open-source-lydia-leong-0ujre/](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/sovereignty-requires-rallying-behind-free-open-source-lydia-leong-0ujre/)  
      Tl;dr; “advocating rallying behind an opinionated FOSS stack.“  
- Alistair: project health includes having support from, or being, a CNA. This requires bridge-building and funding … it’s possible that the long-tail of FOSS projects might get “weeded out” if they don’t receive support  
- Salve objects to this vision of the future, points out the diversity of projects thriving today  
- Greg: if businesses aren’t going to do their diligence, and if regulatory bodies aren’t going to enforce it, then … ???? …. Businesses don’t want to do any more things that cost them money unless they are forced to. It’s prudent for OSS maintainers to be ready, but also not to do more than necessary. Focus on the “MVP” for F/OSS.  
- Greg proposes focusing our output narrowly on the guidance for maintainers who receive CRA-related, or Art.25-related, demand letters. Appreciates Gartner’s push for companies seeking to support digital sovereignty to get more involved upstream  
- Juan: our ability to influence policy directions now will depend on how quickly we can act.  
- Aeva: could we base such a policy recommendation directly on our prior WG work on the light-weight attestation?  
  - Agreement from Juan, Jordan, Timo, Greg  
- Alistair: need to still link the attestation to funding (requests) directly. Perhaps by making economic health of the project visible, perhaps by tracking who has paid \-vs- who is “strip-mining”  
- Jordan: can a manufacturer really have "done due diligence" if the project development isn't funded?  
- Juan: We should also discuss the risk assessment aspect of the CRA. Due diligence is necessarily linked to this. Light weight alone is insufficient to support due diligence. Need to create a link between 3 elements: risk assessment, due diligence, attestation.  
  - Aeva: “Risk assessment” has a specific meaning within these standards. It includes an understanding of the product’s intended use. Considering that most upstream projects do not know, and can not limit, the intended use of products in which they are included, it is impractical to expect an upstream risk assessment to be broadly useful to downstream manufacturing efforts *except for* in special cases. This is also supported by this group’s prior discussions regarding Heavyweight attestations.
